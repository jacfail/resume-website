Undergraduate Senior Capstone Project

CV-Powered Industrial Robotic Packaging and Conveyer System Overhaul

My team and I worked on the redesign of new end effectors and a complete process overhaul of a packaging and conveyor system with two Kawasaki ZX130L robot arms. The two robot end effectors utilize positive and negative pressures to collaboratively assemble and package the payloads to be placed onto an industrial conveyor belt system in the robot cell. The new system also implements low level intelligence in the form of computer vision for increased safety and functionality.

One of my main roles was managing the implementation of intelligence to the system by utilizing two Raspberry Pi 4‚Äôs with connected cameras to detect issues with the placement of the payloads. I accomplished this by utilizing fiducial tags and an image processing algorithm (coded in Python) to detect and verify the position and orientation of the payload.

I utilized modules such as OpenCV, RPI.GPIO, and AprilTags. I was also in charge of completing the final wiring diagram of the system and managing the team as we physically wired up the system.

I have been heavily involved with my peers in the brainstorming, design, 3D modeling, and manufacturing of the physical end effectors. I have also worked extensively with the team on administrative tasks such as a the development of a Gantt chart, project logs, Bill of Materials, and the securing of funding from various sponsors.

Overall, this project integrates many different concepts and theories and is now one of the primary showcases of the Robotics and Mechatronics department at Kennesaw State University. It will provide inspiration and educational value for engineering students for many years to come.
